Common HVAC Questions from Lubbock Homeowners

In Lubbock's climate, twice-yearly service is the practical minimum — spring before heat season and fall before winter. Caliche dust, hard water, and extended high-heat operation mean a single annual tune-up leaves systems under-maintained. Spring service should include coil cleaning, refrigerant check, capacitor test, and condensate drain flush. Annual service agreements from Lubbock HVAC companies typically run $150–$300 for both visits.
HVAC sizing in Lubbock requires a Manual J load calculation — not just square footage. Lubbock's extreme sun exposure and high outdoor temperature differentials often push requirements above the standard 1 ton per 400–600 sq ft rule. An oversized system short-cycles and leaves your home clammy. An undersized system runs continuously on 100°F days, burning out components early. A licensed Lubbock HVAC contractor can perform a Manual J calculation in about 30 minutes.
AC replacement in Lubbock ranges from $4,500–$12,000 installed. A standard 14 SEER2 split system costs $4,500–$7,500. High-efficiency 18–20 SEER2 systems run $7,000–$12,000 but can reduce summer electric bills 20–30% — meaningful when you run AC from April through October. Heat pump systems run $6,000–$10,000 and handle both heating and cooling, popular for Lubbock's mild winters.
The most common Lubbock causes are: failed capacitor (most common on the first 100°F day), low refrigerant from a slow leak, a dirty condenser coil coated in caliche dust, or an undersized system. All four are diagnosable in one service call. If your system is over 12 years old and losing cooling capacity, a new high-efficiency unit often pays for itself in 3–4 years through electric savings.
